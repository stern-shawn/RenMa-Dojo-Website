Kendo and Iaido in Portland, OR

Kendō (剣道), literally the Way of the Sword, is the art of Japanese fencing.

It is one of the oldest and most respected martial arts of Japan, and traces its origins to the techniques and methods used by the samurai to gain mastery of the sword, and ultimately of the mind. Kendo implies a lifelong pursuit of self-knowledge and self-discipline through a demanding physical and mental training regimen.

Begin Your Journey

Both Kendo and Iaido are lifelong pursuits, and there is no better time than now to begin learning the basics. You'd be surprised how much you can learn about swinging a sword and moving your body efficiently.

Start Small

Getting started in Kendo only requires two basic, inexpensive swords (an oak 'bokken' for forms and a bamboo 'shinai' for striking) and comfortable clothes! Iaido only requires the bokken. Kendo and Iaido are both relatively low-cost martial arts over time.

Grow With Others

Train alongside other beginners as well as seasoned upper-level Kenshi. Monthly regional practices bring all local dojos together for intense training and sharing.

Level Up

Kendo training is intense and demanding, and therefore a great environment in which to develop your stamina, mental resilience, and character.
